SUSPENSION


Have you ever wondered what happens after you die? Would you think about the things you could have fixed or just pass on? Suspension is an interactive story about being stuck in limbo with no way out but to face your regrets. You decide the end.







WHAT IS SUSPENSION?


Suspension is an interactive narrative project that transpires over multiple media platforms such as Discord, Instagram, Twine, and AR to stage out the plot of ‘Suspension’. Here, different media platforms are used as ‘artifacts’ to portray the different chapters that make up this interactive experience. The user “plays” out these chapters by interacting with objects placed within the “white museum”. The white museum 3D AR environment acts as the home base of suspension.


The plot of suspension revolves around the anti-hero “Blu”. Through this narrative, users witness their history, behaviors, actions, all the things that make them a pariah. But, it is also a story about redemption, albeit not in a way one expects. Ultimately, the user has left choices they must make based on their views, morals, and strategy. The user decides how Suspension ends.






Meet the Characters


Suspension draws on the relationship between three major characters who intersect, interweave, and incite events throughout the story.Our protagonist "Blu" is an anti-hero who cares more about themselves than others with the exception of their little brother "Finch", the complete opposite of our protagonist and the only figure in the story whom the protagonist shows affection for. Ultimately, when "Death" comes knocking Blu is forced to acknowledge the reason why the looming presence that has been following them in then White Museum.

This plot layout explains the chapters of suspension and the digital artifacts used to depict them.





Prologue | VR Artifact : 3D Museum


The game begins with the user entering the ‘White Museum’. They notice objects and artifacts that remind them of their tainted history. The user has to interact with these objects in order to play out suspension. Players notice a weird looming presence in the white museums. The protagonist is not alone here.






Chapter 1 | Digital Artifact : Webtoon


This chapter explores high school Blu and their close friend Mason’s friendship. Users catch a glimpse of Blu's rocky home environment. Upon considering Blu as close Mason confesses his orientation as gay to Blu. Finals roll around in highschool. Blu asks Mason if they can cheat off him. Mason disagrees and an argument breaks out in the school hallway. During this argument, Mason is outed by Blu in front of all the other students. Blu and Mason reach an unfortunate end. Later on, the aftermath of being outed as gay is shown.

0. The Rabbit Hole — Instagram + Physical Posters


Zappar AR is used as the primary rabbit hole for this Suspension. A physical poster depicting a text from the White Museum accompanied by a scannable QR code shows the user two states of mind— one of regret and one of pure apathy. Once the user scans the marker the artifact encourages them to “Go to the White Museum”. This “coded” strategy was employed to show the duality of mind as well as create an enhanced interactive poster that will surely trick the people to visit the white museum. Secondary Physical Artifact : Instagram

Instagram is used as a secondary rabbit hole to promote Suspension's White Museum. The markeing strategy that is employed here is similar to that of an event promotion. It is ensured that stories and images about the 'white museum experience' are posted often at scheduled times to gather continuous traffic. These posts are often contain rhetorical question thats is intended to make the user wonder about the nature of the white museum, therefore it ensures increased user interest over time. VR Artifact - White Museum


This artifact is the 3D white museum that the user essentially functions within. During the developmental stage, two different layouts of the white museum were tested out to offer a better visual experience. These models were created using Cinema 4D and coded using A-frame. The White Museum uses minimal colors for distinguishing features. As for A-frame some of the primary feathers that are being currently tested out are the Navigations Meshes and user-based object interactions. We are currently trying to establish improved methods within A-frame for smooth transitions between chapters.

Chapter 2. Digital Artifact - Spotify

Within the aforementioned Discord server, a Spotify playlist was integrated to build on Finch and Blu’s world. Since music plays a large role in showcasing a person’s personality through its lyrics and musical style, the intention to add Spotify was to build layers to the characters in this story. The use of a Spotify playlist also establishes “relatable” content so as to increase user investment and worldbuilding. This playlist can be checked out in the discord server or here:




Spotify Playlist↗

Chapter 3. Digital Artifact - Police Case File: Newspaper clipping + Audio File

The police case file narrates the events of Chapter 3 through the means of a pdf. The PDF is a combination of news reports, evidence articles (snippets from discord + audio file), an official accident report and a 911 transcript made to the police. Each page in the police file gives a detailed description of the events leading up to the accident and the accident itself. Within the Police Case file is a ‘black box’ recording of the events that transpired on the day of the accident. The audio gives the listener detailed imagery of the entire day, from the moment Blu and Finch plan to meet, find themselves in troubles, to their final moments together. Chapter 4. Digital Artifact - Twine

In the final chapter Twine is used to break the 4th wall and speak to the player so that they can "play" vicariously through the perspective of Blu. This interaction forges a connection between our anti-hero and the player. It also reinforces the idea that Blu is on their arc of redemption, making them more vulnerable and human in the player’s eyes. Ultimately, the user is offered two options that decide the ending of Suspension. There are no right or wrong endings here and each option leads to a future event involving the chosen option. Closing thoughts



Over the course of this project the team went through multiple rounds of plot and game play interations. After each round we gained more and more insight of what aesthetically worked with the plot and what disrupted the experince in the form of plotholes, media choice, script details etc. We learned that while working on a project we may get desensitized to some of the plotholes and shortcomings in our projects or even our biases might interrupt the user experience. Through this project not only did we gain insignt in to how work better in team settings but also figure out ways to reach common ground where in every one is pleased with the final delivery. We learned how to improve our narrative/ story thinking and accurate transmedia integration. We're grateful for this experince and look forward to apply some of the lessons learned in out future project. Thank you so much for reading. We hope you enjoyed Suspension.
